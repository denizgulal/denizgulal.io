About Me

I am deeply motivated by the mathematical foundations of artificial intelligence, with a particular focus on the theoretical principles that govern learning, robustness, and alignment. I am especially drawn to understanding AI through rigorous, theory-driven approaches, aiming to develop systems whose behavior can be formally characterized and guaranteed. My interest lies in how abstract mathematical structures can inform the design of intelligent systems that are not only effective but also interpretable, reliable, and aligned with human values.

My previous research explored how techniques from applied mathematics—including linear systems theory, spectral methods, optimization, and numerical analysis—can yield principled insights into the dynamics of learning in complex, high-dimensional environments. I am especially interested in how such approaches can inform the theoretical underpinnings of alignment, enabling formal guarantees of stability, interpretability, and reliability in advanced AI systems.

Ultimately, my goal is to contribute to a rigorous theoretical framework for AI alignment,one that unifies insights from mathematics, computer science, and practical domains to ensure that future intelligent systems are not only powerful, but also verifiably safe, trustworthy, and beneficial.
